Pecking Order Theory

Companies prefer internal funds because less subject to information asymmetry, then do external debt, but do not external equity; no such optimum exists, so they do not manipulate leverage.

Market Timing Theory

Firms care neither trade-off nor pecking order; they issue equity when overpriced and repurchase when underpriced; leverage just accumulates this history.

Capital Outflow

Corporate Investment

Only Tobin's q must determine corporate investment, but firms consider cash flows more due to market inefficiency.

  1. Fazzari, Hubbard, and Petersen (1988)
    • CF is more important than q in determining firms' investment; constrained ones (low payout) rely more on CF in particular, while unconstrained ones (high payout) rely more on q (180507).
  2. Stulz (1990)
  3. Lang and Stulz (1994)
  4. Berger and Ofek (1995)
  5. Shin and Stulz (1998)
    • Segments' investment is sensitive more to their own CF and less to others' CF and their q; high q and low q segments are equally sensitive to their CF, so the internal market is inefficient (180507).
  6. Rajan, Servaes, and Zingales (2000)
    • In diversified firms, power game among segments makes the internal market inefficient; profitable segments (high λq, resource×q) underinvest and funds flow toward the poor (low λq) (180507).
  7. Graham, Lemmon, and Wolf (2002)
  8. Billet and Mauer (2003)
    • Efficient subsidies to constrained segments enhance firms' value; inefficient transfer segments negatively affects; the internal market and the diversity does not matter (180507).
  9. Almeida, Campello, and Weisbach (2004)
    • Constrained firms save cash from their CF because they demand liquidity more; they accumulate cash more when they have more investment opportunities (high q) (180507).
  10. Malmendier and Tate (2005)
    • Overconfident CEOs are more sensitive to CF when investing because they think of their stock as underpriced (decrease external finance) and overrate their projects (increase internal finance) (180507).
  11. Ahn, Denis, and Denis (2006)
    • Leverage hurts investment for high-q, non-core segments and does not improve firm value in diversified firms as managerial discretion makes debt inefficient in monitoring agency problems (180507).
  12. Denis and Sibilkov (2010)
    • Cash is more valuable for constrained firms because it allows those with high hedging needs to invest more and investment positively affects stock returns of constrained firms (180507).
  13. Custódio (2014)
    • Acquirers justify their expensive takeover by realizing goodwill as an intangible asset; diversification discount (negative excess q) disappears after goodwill adjustment (180507).
  14. McLean and Zhao (2014)
    • Investment and employment are sensitive more to CF and less to q during recession (costly external finance, low investor sentiment) due to decreased share issuance (not debt issuance) (180507).
