Trade-off Theory

Leverage reduces corporate tax burden and agency cost of equity, but introduces financial distress, debt overhang and agency cost of debt; the best combination minimizes these costs.

Pecking Order Theory

Companies prefer internal funds because less subject to information asymmetry, then do external debt, but do not external equity; no such optimum exists, so they do not manipulate leverage.

  1. Akerlof (1970)
  2. Masuilis (1980)
  3. Myers (1984)
  4. Myers and Majluf (1984)
  5. Titman and Wessels (1988)
  6. Whited (1992)
  7. Shah (1994)
  8. Barclay and Smith (1995)

  10. Fama and French (1998)
  11. Shyam-Sunder and Myers (1999)
  12. Chrinko and Singha (2000)
  13. Fama and French (2002)

Market Timing Theory

Firms care neither trade-off nor pecking order; they issue equity when overpriced and repurchase when underpriced; leverage just accumulates this history.

  1. Baker and Wurgler (2000)
  2. Baker and Wurgler (2002)
